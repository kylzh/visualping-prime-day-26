How we analyzed this

Methodology

We analyzed approximately 2,500 Amazon US products tracked using Visualping's AI page monitoring tool, which records price change events, between May 22 and June 23, 2026.

For this analysis, we focused on approximately 1,070 products that experienced at least one recorded price change during Prime Day (June 23, 2026).

Price data was collected as real-time price change events and stored in a Supabase database.

For each product, we compared its Prime Day price against its historical price records available in the dataset prior to June 23. Products were classified into three categories:

  • New All-Time Low — Prime Day price was lower than any previously observed price in the dataset.
  • Matches Previous Low — Prime Day price matched the lowest previously observed price.
  • Not Lowest Price — Prime Day price was higher than a previously observed low price.

Products with no recorded price history prior to June 23 could not be evaluated against historical prices and were excluded from this comparison. This analysis reflects observed price changes within our tracking dataset and does not represent Amazon's full historical pricing behavior.
